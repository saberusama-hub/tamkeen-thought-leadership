<p style={{ marginTop: 8, marginBottom: 8 }}><strong>Four facts that explain most rank-gap surprises:</strong></p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Reputation surveys mean-revert slowly.</strong> Big reputation gains take years to register. Big losses take just as long to disappear.</li>
  <li><strong>Citation indicators move faster.</strong> A research-intensive institution can climb the citation pillar in 3–5 years; QS rewards that less than THE does.</li>
  <li><strong>Asian research universities read strongly to THE.</strong> Long-established US/UK names read more strongly to QS. The same institution sits in two different places on the two lists, by design.</li>
  <li><strong>The 12 largest divergences in 2026</strong> all fall into one of the two structural buckets above. None are noise.</li>
</ul>

<SectionHeader
  number="09"
  kicker="Methodology & sources"
  title="How we built the dataset."
/>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Coverage.</strong> QS Top 500, 2017–2026 (ten editions). THE Top 500, 2016–2026 (eleven editions). 1,038 distinct canonical institutions across 96 countries; 10,536 institution-year-system rows.</li>
  <li><strong>Rank handling.</strong> Integer ranks pass through unchanged. Banded ranks (e.g., "401–500") converted to numerical midpoints with the original band retained for tier-boundary analysis. Tie markers stripped.</li>
  <li><strong>Country normalisation.</strong> ISO-3166 alpha-3 via pycountry, plus manual aliases for Hong Kong, Macao, Taiwan, Iran, Korea, Russia, the UAE, and other commonly inconsistent entries. Greater China (China + Hong Kong + Macao + Taiwan) retained as a separate analytical view.</li>
  <li><strong>Institution matching.</strong> Three rules in order: hard alias for known cases; exact match on aggressively-normalised name; controlled fuzzy match (token_sort_ratio ≥ 97 AND first significant token agrees AND length ratio ≥ 0.75). All fuzzy edges audited.</li>
  <li><strong>2024 methodology break flagging.</strong> Year-over-year rank changes computed only for integer-ranked rows. The 2024 breaks (THE WUR 3.0; QS revision) are flagged in the data; cross-year analyses straddling the break are discussed methodology-first throughout. Validated against a 28-institution truth panel with anti-collision tests.</li>
</ul>
